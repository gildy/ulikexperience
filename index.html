ULIK - Notes on User Experience

Putting User at the Core of Our Product Design to Provide the Best Possible Experience

Notes on our user centred thought process in creating ULIK.

Why Are We Doing This UX Thing?

  • We want to make our customers 'feel good' about the product they are going to be using
  • We want them to actively engage with the product in the long run
  • We want to make a good image of our brand 'ULIK', as well as the company: 'Stream Intelligence' in a wider scope

Seriously though, why does it matter?

What do we mean by 'feel good': Trust, Engagement, Satisfaction

Our product handles a lot of sensitive data from the users. We want to make sure that they are comfortable on trusting us to analyse the data, while providing the best possible experience on helping them grow their business.

So What is ULIK? What Do We Want Our Product to Be?

Our vision is to build a tool that makes excellent use of data, metrics, and social media trend to provide compelling insights in helping small business owners to grow

Our target market is small-medium sized online clothing stores which engage on social media (Instagram or Facebook) to connect with their audience

Our business goals are New Revenue Stream and Stronger Company Branding for Stream Intelligence.

“People hire our product to do the job of getting insights and knowing necessary actions to boost their sales when they are too busy running their business. ” — Our Product Manager

“ The other applicants for this job are Holistics, SAP, and Style Sage, but our product will always get their attention because we understand every important aspect of running small & medium-sized business. ” — Our Product Manager

Phase 1

User Acquisition: Convince, Persuade, and Build Trust From Potential Customers

Things From Potential Customers We Have to be Able to Answer:

  1. What is ULIK and Why Do I Need This?
  2. Why are Data and Metrics Important?
  3. Who is Stream Intelligence? Why Should I Sign Up and Give My Data to This Company?

Potential cusomers may not be aware about the importance of data and metrics to help grow their business. Even if they do, their current data may not be structured well enough or sufficient to be analysed. They do not have enough reason to sign up.

Do we understand them?

Our Approach for Phase 1

Publish

Release articles on growing business using data. Giving potential users understanding about the importance of data and metrics.

Persuade

Convince them to sign up for the articles and trying not to invoke that "we're trying to sell something". This way, they may have less hesitation for signing up.

Gain Trust

Gain User Trust by introducing who we are, what we do, who did we collaborate.

Explain

Provide Clear and Concise landing page: Easy to understand, straightforward, and on point. Giving them the exact idea on what value are they going to get by signing up with us.

Research To Do: Getting Even Deeper Understanding on Our Users Behaviour

Big Question: What are they up to?

Qualitative Approach

What is their current behaviour in running their online business through social media?

How do they make their contents? Do they consider about their competitors? How do they communicate with their customers?

Methodology: Thematic analyis on potential user interviews and their online activity observations

Quantitative Approach

How does the social media engagement (likes, comments, followers) relates to the actual sales and/or growth of the business?

Can we suggest that improving social media engagement can actually lead to better business?

Methodology: Statistical data analysis

First Phase Deliverables: Landing Page

What do we want in our landing page?

  1. Simple, clear, concise, and on point wordings
  2. Friendly explanation as our target may not be familiar with corporate/technical terms
  3. Tell the user clearly what they will exactly get by using the product. (As simple as: Efektif untuk membuat jualan instagram cepat laku! or Bisa tambah likes dan follower toko anda!)
  4. Tell the user how good our product is and why they should use it
  5. Give user options to get to know more about the product: Watching video or try the demo without the cost of signing up
  6. Good summary of everything and call to action
  7. FAQ section. Explaining in more details, but still crystal clear, about doubts and questions that the potential users might have. This is crucial especially when the targeted users are not familiar with the technology that we are going to provide

User Study: End of July 2017

What Worked:

  • Intuitive
  • Clear Message. Pretty much explaining what is offered
  • Clean look.
  • Tailored for fashion
  • Good user flow
  • Giving a sense of the most promising feature: Trends, Segmentation and also Forecasting, which helps to grow business

What Did Not Work:

  • Data? What data? What kind of data? Is it my customer? The transaction?: learn more section will be helpful.
  • "As long as I don't know what kind of data do you mean, I will not start"
  • No video explanation
  • Does not appeal enough to make me sign up.
  • Why should I trust you and the company to handle my sensitive data?
  • What do you mean by social graph/analysis?

User Study: End of July 2017

What Did Not Work:

  • The Wordings!
  • What is this? What do I do? What is this for?
  • It needs a straightforward explanation on what is happening now
  • How does this affect/ improve my sales?

Another Bits from the user study:

  • The hassle is not on the data processing, but it is on data collecting
  • Manual input, difficult to collect
  • Using other platforms' tools for the purpose: e.g: Sirclo. Analyse monthly.

What's next? Final verdict for the study result:

  • More guidance about data! How can we actually help them to collect the 'right' data?
  • When they have more understanding about data, they can 'feel' and 'understand' more about our product, and what's going on inside the dashboard
  • Video OR Onboarding tutorial. Step by step. What do I do here?
  • Introduce ourself! Who we are? Who did we work with? Show them that they can trust their data on us
  • STRAIGHTFORWARD, CLEAR, and CONCISE on every single part of the dashboard
  • More research about other company's tools and position ourself
